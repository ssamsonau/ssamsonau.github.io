Bagging and Boosting in Human learning

2019-03-22

Just a funny thought: education in humanities follows bagging trees, education in natural sciences follows boosting. Here is why..

Think about this..

Humanities:

  • Read a book. Understand precisely what an author is saying, understand his/her model/view of reality/world
    • Fit a tree to get a simple model. Build a fully grown tree (don’t prune)
  • Read another book. Get another view of reality. Ideally, this shall be done without any filters of perception which may have been learned from the 1st book
    • Fit another tree
  • Read many more books
    • Fit many trees
  • Make up your mind based on conglomerations of all those ideas and some other experiences
    • Use an aggregation of trees as a final model

In both cases, there is a high variance of views from book to book (from model to model), and ideally low bias (deep understanding of every book). Aggregation helps reduce variance in both cases.

Natural sciences (for example Physics)

  • Read one book/take intro class. Understand it as much as you can on the first run
    • fit shallow tree
  • Address what you did not understand from the first run. Try to understand those things which are still not clear
    • fit another shallow tree on the residuals
  • Continue the refinement of the knowledge
    • fit many more shallow trees, every time on residuals of previous models

In this case, every new iteration never (almost) makes understanding (model) learned (obtained) on previous iterations wrong, and mostly fine tunes those.

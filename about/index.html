My interests include Physics, Data Science, Education.

In education I like designing a simplest possible way to deliver an idea, and create conditions for natural further development.

This blog presents a simplissimost introduction (as I see it) to topics or ideas. And some other stuff from time to time.

Short story

  • For my Master’s degree (theoretical Physics) I was playing with a behavior of a particle transitioning between potential wells. This was addressed using computer simulations in Matlab and Wolfram Mathematica.

  • Later I got interested in psychology, participated in some educational classes, which now, probably, influences my teaching a lot.

  • Then I went to work in IT, playing with algorithms for stock market trading (mostly using C).

  • For PhD degree (Physics) I was working on studying a growth mechanism of graphene-like (1 layer carbon film) films on a quartz substrate. Part of work - experiment, part - computer simulation using Quantum Espresso and CUNY high performance computer center resources.

  • After teaching Physics at City University of New York for several years I formed an impression, that introducing Physics to students at a college level may be much less efficient comparing to do doing that at a High School (I was introduced to Physics at 9th grade, and had some basis for comparison).

    • So I went to work at a High School to teach Physics. I focused on labs for core classes (algebra- and calculus-based) and on Quantum Mechanics
    • It appeared that having an opportunity to introduce research as part of a Physics curriculum is really beneficial for general kids’ understanding on how Physics works
    • At the high school I was (and am) working on 1) teaching kids stuff 2) preparing them to do research 3) mentoring them in their research activities
    • I did organize and do mentor lab on micro- and nano- technology
    • Quantum Mechanics class follows a college level book, starting with Dirac notation at the beginning - much better way comparing to wave mechanics (from my teaching experience)

  • While teaching Physics I also started learning/doing Data Science.

    • Coursera classes, projects on Kaggle, books, projects, etc
    • In 2016 I and a colleague organized a training program for PhDs at Columbia University
    • In 2017 I started teaching a full year class of Data Science for kids at a High School (see teaching page).
    • I also spend some time working as a consultant at NYC firms.

At this moment

I continue to teach Physics (theory, experiment, computer simulations), and Data Science, while also doing some projects and consulting as time allows

Generally speaking, my interests are in the area of learning - human learning and machine learning :)

  • Human learning
    • I have been teaching since 2009. I worked with students from middle school, high school, college, and graduate school levels
    • How do people learn? What are the most efficient ways? What is the measure of success in learning?
  • Machine learning, AI (and generally data science)
    • Data exploration, reshaping, understanding, modeling
    • An alternative (to science) way to make models of the world
    • Can a machine learn as effectively as a human? Or even more effectively?
  • Psychology of learning
    • What makes people learn better
    • How to communicate (transfer information) with people more efficiently

Background

At this moment I do consulting projects in data science and teach physics, data science, as well as mentor research in data science and experimental physics. I worked on the development of new types of curriculum for science education.

Tools I have experience with

Data science, machine learning, programming

  • Spark, H2O for big data

Experimental and computational physics

CVD, MBE, photo-lithography, plasma and etching, sputtering, processing in a clean room environment, AFM, SEM, spectroscopy, photo-lithography.

Wolfram Mathematica, MATLAB, Density Functional Theory (DFT), Quantum ESPRESSO, high-performance computing (HPC)
